pg. 17
Eye contact with O'Brien
"But there was a fraction of a second when their eyes met, and for as long as it took to happen Winston knew - yes he knew! - that O'Brien was thinking the same thought." (p. 17)
Eye contact with O'Brien is Winston's first indication that something may be amiss within the party. It gives him hope that he may not be forever resigned to this fate.
pg. 75
Rutherford and real evidence
"There is hope. I know it. I feel it." (p. 75)
The only concrete proof that the party changes information of the past that Winston has ever seen is the photo of Rutherford. He feels that if he knows this, surely others will also figure it out soon and there can be a revolution.
pg. 97
Finding the room
"The place looked curiously inviting. The thought flitted through Winston's mind that it would probably be quite easy to rent the room for a few dollars a week, if he dared to take the risk." (p. 97)
Finding the room above the shop gives Winston a place where he feels safe from the party. He knew it would be risky, given that it would be a crime against the party to have private thoughts, so he had to take time to consider the decision.
pg. 108
Julia gives him the note
"Then the memory of her face came back, and withit, intolerable desire to be alone" (p. 108)
Julia giving him the note gave him two things. A love worth fighting for, and someone to confide in. He finally felt true love, which to him was enough to keep fighting. It also gave him someone to fight with.
pg. 137
Winston rents the room
"Winston looked round the shabby little room above Mr. Charrington's shop. It was little to behold, but to him, it did not matter." (p. 137)
With exeption to the purchase of the diary, renting the room is the first non-thoughtcrime Winston commits. It shows that his plans have gone from thoughts to actions, indicating progress in his mission.
pg. 167
Meeting with O'brien
"They had done it, they had done it at last, was all he could think. It had been a rash act to come here at all, and sheer folly to arrive together" (p. 167)
The meeting with O'Brien gives Winston what he has been yearning for for so long: A plan. He finally had someone to coordinate his actions with, and it brought him great pleasure. He also got the book from the meeting, so he could learn more about the history of the plan to dismantle the party.
pg. 184
Reading the book
"We must read it. We Must. It is essential."
Reading the book enlightened Winston as to how he could actually overthrow the party. Much to his enjoyment, because it was the idea he had initially suggested, the book said that the power to overthrow the party lay in the Proletariats.
pg. 221
They get caught by thought police
"It was starting, it was starting at last! They could do nothing except stand gazing into one another's eyes" (p. 221)
Getting caught by the thought police is the start of the downfall of Winston's plan. Naturally, when an all powerful dictatorship catches on to your plan to overthrow it they tend to be unhappy. Getting caught also seperates Winston from Julia, which kept Winston away from the one reason he was still fighting.
pg. 286
Winston Betrays Julia
"Do it to Julia! Do it to Julia! Not to me! Julia!" (p. 286)
Winston betraying Julia marked the end of Winston's fight. Up until this point, even after being tortured mercilessly by the party, Winston had still kept fighting because of his love for Julia. By giving up Julia, he gave up his reason to keep fighting. after this, he no longer resists the torture and mind control from Winston.
pg. 298
Winston Caves In
"He had won the victory over himself. He loved Big Brother." (p. 298)
Winston caves in and finally admits that he loves Big Brother, indicating that the party had finally won.
